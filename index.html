with the open call "Spaceship Earth"
we invite artists, designers, scientists, writers and visual storytellers to imagine the most daring, most promising, most inspiring new ideas about the changing relationship between humans, nature and technology.
Thought-provoking ideas that demonstrate a clear link to either the biosphere, the technosphere or the mindsphere.
Unconventional ways, yet tangible experiences that invite the audience to discover, unfold and engage with the next stages of evolution; the project must include an interactive component in which the audience can discover, learn and grow in their own personal ways.
Innovating projects that apply methods, approaches or tools from (speculative) design and (bio)technology; machine learning; synthetic biology; biomimicry; robotics

WHO CAN

Artists, designers, and visual storyteller with keen interest in socio-cultural implications of technology, who graduated in the field of art or design (at either the master's or bachelor's level). You can participate individually, or with other. The development fee will stay the same (25.000 euros), regardless of the amount of creatives in your team. Both EU and non-EU citizen may submit an application. For non-EU citizens it is necessary to take travel expenses, visa rules and COVID-19 regulations into consideration as they need to be covered from the budget or otherwise taken care of by themselves.

WHAT TO

Each participant can submit only one application for this call. The application consist of:

- Completed & signed application form including project idea.

- Curriculum Viate (CV).

- Portfolio of previous work (max 20 slides).

UPLOAD

Upload PDF files of your completed application form, your CV and portfolio You can convert Word documents with a PDF converter The maximum file size for each of these individual files cannot be exceed 4MB.

If you did not receive a confirmation by email (even though on the website it state your application was successful), it's likely an error occurred. If so, please contact us via opencall@nextnature.net.

Development fee Four candidates / teams will be selected and receive a development fee of €25.000 each.

JURY

From all submisions, a selection committe of Next Nature will preselect 16 projects that demonstrate a clear link to Spaceship Earth. The selection committe will take into consideration the relevance to the theme, the potential for interaction with an audience, and application of technology.

The jury consist of :

- Rob van Hatuum (chair) Chief Science Officer NEMO, VPRO TV maker

- Caroline Breunesse Director of Exhibitions Naturalis

- Lin-Lin Chen Design Professor and Dean Industrial Design Dept./ TU Eindhoven.

SPECIAL

Special conditions that apply to the award :

The development fee of €25.000, combined with the timeframe of 6 months, is sufficient to realize a first prototype of the project> If the prototype is successful, we continue to project into final realization. Emerging (bio)technology must not be crucial for the realization of the project. Backup solutions should be available.

IF SELECTED

The designer/artist is available for the kickoff event in June 2023. Physical presence is not mandatory. The designer/artist is available for the first feedback round that takes place in September 2023. Physical presence is not mandatory. The designer/artist is available for the second feedback that takes place in November 2023, followed by demonstration of the prototype during a public event. Physical presence is mandatory, at the expense of the project. Any visa requirements are to be dealt with by the designer/artist.

DEADLINE

Friday 31 March 2023 (deadline 23.59 CET)

OBJECTIVES

This open call aims to simulate artists, designers, scientists, writers and visual storyteller to imagine the most daring, most promising, most inspiring new ideas about the changing relationship between humans, nature, and technology. We challenge them to dream of a more inclusive and sustainable world. A world in which biology and technology merge. A world in which humans and planet enter a new evolutionary phase. A world woth living in.
